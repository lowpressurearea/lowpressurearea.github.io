GEUSELF made me realize that all of us are in the constant cycle of learning and finding genuinity, authenticity, and our role in the world we live in. Now, I would like to discuss "myself" in the form of a "Narrative Knowing". The first concept I would like to discuss would be the first perspective of the self: The Philosophical perspective, specifically Heidegger's & Satre's concept of Authenticity. Authenticity is one factor and this is about knowing oneself by being true and honest with yourself and others, exhibiting credibility in words and actions, and avoiding pretenses. In terms of my progress in this perspective, I think I'm still trying to "narrow down" and pursue what is next on what I truly want. A term left before I become a 3rd year, I would say that I'm in the right degree but still unsure with what's next for me after my academics/career path. I think authenticity doesn't just end with doing what you love currently but also having a vision for the future (which I'm still figuring out). The next perspective I would like to discuss is the sociological perspective. Based on Callero's paper (2003), the concept of social constructs specifically about identity, I was able to examine the societal norms and institutional influences that impact my sense of self. Influences don't just end in the four corners of a classroom and are in fact brought by which "herd" one is following with. One thing I would also like to add up as well is the concept of power in society. Personally, I was more immersed in activism in college than when I was in highschool. Contemplating power dynamics and reflexivity within the sociological perspective encourages me to critically assess the power structures and social hierarchies that exist, making my mind open and aware of the injustices in our surroundings which are set by our own government's incompetencies. The last perspective I would like to discuss would be the Oriental perspective. A little share on my end (hehe) but I am slowly trying to incorporate the concept of the 5 skandhas in my life for my well-being. I remember it being discussed and I voluntarily read more about it out of curiosity. It has allowed me to cultivate a deeper sense of self-awareness and mindfulness in my experiences. I find my philosophical self and my oriental self coinciding in my decisions.
